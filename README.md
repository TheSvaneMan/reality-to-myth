## 🌿 The Vision

> *"This work aims to redefine our relationship with nature by fostering kinship with plants, emphasizing not just their preservation but transforming our connection to nurture respect and acknowledge their agency in the ecological community."*

**Reality-to-Myth** is a digital-ecological experiment designed to bridge the gap between human perception and plant agency. It serves as a platform to acknowledge plants not as "resources," but as active participants and kin within our shared ecological community.

---

## 🏗️ Technical Composition (Legacy)

Based on the repository structure, this project was built using a modern JavaScript stack focused on dynamic rendering and user participation:

- **Framework:** Next.js (as seen in `layout.js`, `page.js`, and `not-found.js`)
- **Data:** JSON-driven architecture (`countries.json`) for global participation.
- **Styling:** Global CSS with custom animations (`globals.css`).
- **Core Modules:**
  - `experience/`: Optimised assets for immersion.
  - `plant/[id]`: Dynamic routing to individual plant profiles.
  - `map/`: Geospatial rendering of participants.
  - `userForm.js`: Interface for users to register their connection to the network.

---

## 🗺️ How it Functioned

Participants could navigate through an interactive experience that mapped human-plant connections across borders, using data-driven visuals to render the "agency" of the botanical world in a way the human eye could finally acknowledge.

---

## 🔄 Reflections on Kinship

The project moved through several iterative phases, from the initial `init` and `Create Next App` boilerplate to complex `Render participants` logic that visualized the growing ecological community. The "Reality" of our current separation from nature was intended to be transformed into a new "Myth"—a story where humans and plants are once again intertwined.
